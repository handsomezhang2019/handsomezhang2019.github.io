A Timeline of Modern Shanghai
上海近代歷史年表

June 1843

Treaty of Nanjing
《南京條約》

Shanghai became one of the four treaty ports opened by the Qing government for foreign trade in the wake of the First Opium War (1839-42).

November 1845

Establishment of British Settlement
英租界建立

Land was given to British merchants in Shanghai to build a settlement.

November 1848

Birth of American Settlement
美租界誕生

After negotiating with the Chinese governer of the area, American merchants also estalished.

April 1849

Founding of French Concession
法租界建立

The Shanghai French Concession was estalished, when the French Consul to Shanghai, Charles de Montigny, obtained a proclamation from the Chinese governer of Shanghai, which conceded certain territory for a French settlement.

September 1853

The Small Sword Society
小刀會起義 

A group of rebels called The Small Sword Society occupied the Chinese-held old city of Shanghai. Numbers of Chinese refugees fled into the foreign settlements. 

1860-1862

The Taiping Heveanly Kingdom
太平天國

The occupation of surrounding areas by the rebels of Taiping led to another wave of Chinese immigration into the settlements. 

September 1863

The Merging
英美租界合併

The British and American settlements merged into one, known as the Shanghai International Settlement. 

November 1937-December 1941

The Lone Island
“孤島”上海

Surrounded by the Imperial Japanese Army, who had occupied the Chinese territory in Shanghai since late 1937, The two settlements entered what was later known as the "Lone Island" period. 

1943

The "Return"
“交還”租界

The foreign settlements were nominally given back to China by British and American governments, and by Vichi France, respectively.

August 1945 

The Surrender of Japan
日本投降

As Japan surrendered to U.S.A and China and Japanese troop retreated, the Nationalist government of China was finally able to reclaim sovereignty over the territories of the two settlements. 

May 1949

The Communist Takeover
中共接管

The People Liberation Army of the Chinese Communist Party gained control of Shanghai as the CCP emerged victorious in the Chinese Civil War (1945-1949). The administrative status of Shanghai has not changed since then.
